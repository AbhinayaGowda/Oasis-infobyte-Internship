The People's President

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam was a visionary scientist, educator and the 11th President of India. Known as the Missile Man of India, he played a crucial role in India's space and defense programs.

Despite reaching the highest office, he remained humble, simple, and deeply connected with students. His life is a symbol of dedication, integrity, and service to the nation.

Dr. Kalam believed that youth are the real strength of the country and dedicated his life to inspiring young minds to dream big and work hard.

The Journey of a Legend

Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Dr. Kalam came from a humble background. Through dedication and discipline, he became one of India’s greatest scientists.

He led India's missile development programs and played a key role in the Pokhran nuclear tests.

As President, he transformed Rashtrapati Bhavan into a people's house and became an icon of hope.
